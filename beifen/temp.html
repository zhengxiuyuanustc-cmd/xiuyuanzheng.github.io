About Me

I am a junior undergraduate student at USTC, majoring in Theoretical and Applied Mechanics with a minor in Computer Science. My research interests lie at the intersection of computational mechanics, scientific computing, and machine learning. I am currently exploring adaptive mesh generation and deep learning methods for fracture prediction.

Research Projects

Adaptive Mesh Generation for Complex Tire Structures
C++ | Advancing Front Method | FEA

Developed a C++ program to automatically generate high-quality finite element meshes for complex tire geometries. Implemented local feature extraction algorithms to identify high-stress regions for adaptive refinement.

Neural Network-Enhanced Adaptive Mesh Optimization
PyTorch | Python | COMSOL

Exploring the integration of neural networks to predict optimal mesh density distributions, aiming to automate and optimize the pre-processing pipeline for finite element simulations.
